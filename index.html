Cosmology: The Origin and Evolution of Everything

Introduction

Cosmology is usually defined as the origin and evolution of the universe.

Overview

The current consensus model of the origins of the universe is known as lambda-CDM, where CDM stands for Cold Dark Matter. The foundation of the model is the Big Bang, where space-time, matter and energy came into being from nothing... or possibly quantum field fluctuations.

In the 1980s, Alan Guth and others developed Inflationary Theory, which was designed to solve problems with the smoothness and homogeneity of the universe, and while it remains controversial, it is part of the standard model of cosmology.
